What is Virtual Reality?

Virtual reality (VR) is a believable, and interactive computer-generated experience that is immersive and explorable and engages both the body and the mind.

The technology that makes Virtual Reality (VR) possible is a headset, also known as Head-mounted display (HMD). The head-mounted display makes it possible to view 3D images that can adapt to your head-movements in real-time. HDMs consist of two small screens, one in front of each eye, stereo headphones; thus, eliminating all distractions from the real world surrounding the user. The two screens display stereoscopic images, adapted to each individual eye, which creates a realistic 3D perspective of the virtual world.

HDMs have built-in position sensors that can detect the position and orientation of the user’s head, and adjust the picture accordingly. To make the VR experience even more interactive, advanced Haptic Systems are used (primarily in medical and gaming applications), and they incorporate tactile information, known as force feedback through the transmission of vibrations and other sensations.
